## 📘 Steganography in Video: Concealing Information in Motion
*Subject: Computer Science, Information Security, Multimedia Engineering | Pages: 1-8*

Video steganography is a powerful technique for covert communication, embedding secret data within video files to remain imperceptible to casual observation. This field leverages the inherent structure of video, particularly the sequence of frames, to hide information without altering the video's visual appearance. Understanding the principles of video compression, including I-frames and P-frames, is crucial for effective steganographic techniques.

**Video Structure and Compression** — Video is a sequence of still images (frames) compressed using techniques like I-frames and P-frames to reduce file size and improve efficiency.

**Steganographic Embedding Techniques** — Various methods are employed to embed data into video frames, ranging from simple pixel manipulation to more complex algorithms that exploit video characteristics.

- Steganography allows for covert communication by hiding data within seemingly innocuous media.
- Understanding video compression formats (I-frames, P-frames) is essential for designing effective steganographic algorithms.
- PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio): A metric used to evaluate the quality of a stego-image after embedding, indicating the level of distortion introduced.

## 📑 Section Summaries

### pages 1-8 — Video Steganography

Video steganography is the art of concealing data within video files without altering their apparent visual characteristics. This is achieved by embedding secret information into the video frames, making it undetectable to the naked eye but recoverable with specialized tools. Video is a sequence of still images (frames) that, when displayed in rapid succession, create the illusion of motion. I-frames are self-contained frames, while P-frames use predictive coding to improve efficiency.

**Key Concepts:**
- Video Steganography — A method of hiding secret data within a video file without altering its apparent visual characteristics.
- Frame — A single still image that constitutes a video.
- I-frame — An intra-coded frame that is self-contained and does not require references to other frames.
- P-frame — A predictive-coded frame that uses forward prediction from preceding I- or P-frames.
