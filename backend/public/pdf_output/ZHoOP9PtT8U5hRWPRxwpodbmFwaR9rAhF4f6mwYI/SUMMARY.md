## 📘 Steganography in Video: Concealing Secrets in Moving Images
*Subject: Computer Science, Cryptography, Multimedia Security | Pages: 1-4*

Video steganography is a powerful technique for concealing sensitive information within video files, ensuring data security and privacy. By embedding data into the visual components of video frames, it allows for imperceptible data transmission. This approach leverages the inherent structure of video, which is composed of a sequence of images, to hide data without altering the video's apparent content. The goal is to make the hidden information undetectable to casual observation while still enabling its recovery using specialized tools.

**Steganographic Techniques** — This explores various methods for embedding data into video frames, including pixel value manipulation, spatial arrangement modification, and transform domain techniques.

**Perceptual Hiding** — Focuses on ensuring the hidden data does not significantly impact the visual quality of the video, maintaining its natural appearance.

**Extraction Methods** — Covers the techniques used to recover the embedded data from the video file, including statistical analysis, image processing, and machine learning approaches.

- Understand the fundamental principles of video steganography and its applications.
- Be able to identify and analyze different steganographic techniques used in video files.
- Steganographic Capacity: The maximum amount of data that can be hidden in a video file without being detected.

## 📑 Section Summaries

### pages 1-4 — Video Steganography

Video steganography is the art of concealing secret data within video files without altering the video's apparent content. This is achieved by embedding data into video frames, making the hidden information imperceptible to the naked eye while still allowing its extraction using specialized tools. The core principle relies on the fact that video is a sequence of images (frames), each a grid of pixels, and that data can be hidden within the pixel values or spatial arrangement of these pixels.

**Key Concepts:**
- Steganography — The practice of concealing a message within another message, such as a video file, to avoid detection.
- Video Frame — A single image within a video sequence, representing a specific moment in time.
- Blob — A set of connected pixels within a video frame that represent a hidden data element.
