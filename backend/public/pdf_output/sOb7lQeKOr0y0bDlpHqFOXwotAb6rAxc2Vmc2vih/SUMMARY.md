## 📘 Steganography in Video: Concealing Information in Motion
*Subject: Computer Science, Information Security, Multimedia Engineering | Pages: 1-10*

Video steganography is a powerful technique for covert communication, embedding secret data within video files to remain imperceptible to casual observation. This field leverages the inherent structure of video, particularly the sequence of frames, to hide information without altering the video's visual appearance. Understanding the principles of video compression, including I-frames and P-frames, is crucial for effective steganographic techniques. This course explores the theoretical foundations, practical implementation, and security considerations of video steganography.

**Video Compression** — Explores the different types of video compression, including I-frames, P-frames, and B-frames, and how they impact steganographic embedding and extraction.

**Steganographic Techniques** — Covers various steganographic methods used to embed data into video frames, including spatial domain, transform domain, and frequency domain techniques, along with their strengths and weaknesses.

- Understand the principles of video compression and their impact on steganographic embedding.
- Be able to implement and evaluate different video steganographic techniques.
- PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio): A metric used to evaluate the quality of a stego image after embedding, indicating the amount of distortion introduced.

### Conceptual Flow
The course begins with an introduction to video compression and the fundamental concepts of I-frames, P-frames, and B-frames. This foundation is then used to explore various steganographic techniques, starting with simple methods and progressing to more advanced approaches. The course concludes with a discussion of security considerations and the challenges of detecting and removing stego data.

### Practical Relevance
Video steganography has applications in secure communication, data protection, and covert information dissemination. It can be used to transmit sensitive data through video streams, making it difficult for adversaries to detect the presence of hidden information.

## 📑 Section Summaries

### pages 1-8 — Video Steganography

Video steganography is the art of concealing data within video files without altering their apparent visual characteristics. This is achieved by embedding secret information into the video frames, making it undetectable to the naked eye but recoverable with specialized tools. Video is a sequence of still images (frames) that, when displayed in rapid succession, create the illusion of motion. I-frames are self-contained frames, while P-frames use predictive coding to improve efficiency.

**Key Concepts:**
- Video Steganography — A method of hiding secret data within a video file without altering its apparent visual characteristics.
- Frame — A single still image that constitutes a video.
- I-frame — An intra-coded frame that is self-contained and does not require references to other frames.
- P-frame — A predictive-coded frame that uses forward prediction from preceding I- or P-frames.
