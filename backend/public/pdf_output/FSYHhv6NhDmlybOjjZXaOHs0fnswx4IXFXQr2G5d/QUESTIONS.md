### pages 9-10 — B-Frame


#### 🔤 MCQ

**Q1** `🟢 L2` *(explain)*  [2 marks]

> What are B-frames in video compression?

<details><summary>Model Answer</summary>

B-frames are frames in a video clip that are produced by the encoder using both forward and backward reference to previous/next I or Pframes.

</details>

**Q4** `🟢 L2` *(explain)*  [2 marks]

> What is the main difference between I frames and P/B frames?

<details><summary>Model Answer</summary>

I frames contain the full information from the image, while P/B frames only record the differences between the current frame and the previous/next frames.

</details>

**Q7** `🟢 L2` *(explain)*  [2 marks]

> What is the role of the encoder in creating B-frames?

<details><summary>Model Answer</summary>

The encoder uses both forward and backward references to previous/next I or Pframes to produce B-frames.

</details>

#### 💡 Concept

**Q3** `🟢 L2` *(explain)*  [2 marks]

> What is the purpose of using forward and backward references in B-frames?

<details><summary>Model Answer</summary>

To reduce the amount of data that needs to be stored and transmitted, by only storing the differences between the current frame and the previous/next frames.

</details>

**Q5** `🟢 L2` *(explain)*  [2 marks]

> What is the benefit of using B-frames in video compression?

<details><summary>Model Answer</summary>

B-frames reduce the amount of data that needs to be stored and transmitted, leading to smaller file sizes and faster transmission rates.

</details>
